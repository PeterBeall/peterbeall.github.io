Here are some courses I have taken.

Introduction to Number Theory

Divisibility, Division Algorithm, primes, Euclidean Algorithm, Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic, Fermat numbers, Binomial Theorem, congruences, Euler's Theorem, Wilson's Theorem, sums of two squares, Chinese Remainder Theorem, Hensel's Lemma, quadratic residues, Euler's Criteria, Gauss's Lemma, Quadratic Reciprocity Law of Gauss

The textbook was An Introduction to the Theory of Numbers, 5th edition by Ivan Niven, Herbert S. Zuckerman, and Hugh L. Montgomery. Material covered includes sections 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 1.4, 2.1, 2.2, 2.3, 2.6, 2.7, 2.8, 3.1, 3.2, and 3.6.

Elementary Differential Equations

First-order differential equations (separable equations, linear equations, special integrating factors, transformations), applications of first-order equations, linear second-order differential equations (homogenous equations, nonhomogenous equations), laplace transform, inverse laplace transform, convolution, Dirac delta function, power series solutions

The textbook was Fundamentals of Differential Equations and Boundary Value Problems, 7th edition by R. Nagle, Edward Saff, and Arthur Snider. Material covered includes sections 1.1 to 1.4, 2.2 to 2.6, 4.1 to 4.7, 7.2 to 7.6, 7.8, 7.9, and 8.1 to 8.4.

Data Structures and Algorithms

Algorithm analysis, lists, stacks, queues, trees, tree traversals, balanced trees, heaps, priority queues, sorting, sets, maps, graphs, greedy algorithms, dynamic programming, complexity theory

Calculus III

Vector algebra, Euclidean spaces, geometry of lines and planes in space, basic theory of quadric surfaces, vector functions and curves in space, basic geometry of curves in space (tangent vector, curvature, and torsion), functions of several variables, limits and continuity, differentiability and partial derivatives, extreme values of a function of several variables, the method of Lagrange multipliers, Riemann integration theory, multiple and repeated integrals, transformations, Jacobian of transformation, change of variables in multiple integrals, integrals over curves and surfaces, improper multiple integrals, vector fields, conservative vector fields, line integrals of a vector field, flux of a vector field, Green’s and Stokes’ theorems, the divergence (Gauss-Ostrogradsky) theorem

The textbook was Concepts in Calculus III by S.V. Shabanov. Material covered includes the entire textbook.
